Part 2: Engineering Superiority

The Physics of Reliability

1

5-Axis Active Magnetic Bearing (AMB)

Unlike mechanical bearings, AMB creates a zero-friction environment. The rotor is suspended in a magnetic field, controlled by high-speed DSPs (20kHz sampling).

Benefit: Theoretically infinite bearing life; No lubrication maintenance.
2

High-Strength Alloy Steel Rotor

Chosen over Carbon Fiber for cost-efficiency and safety. While energy density is lower, Power Density (crucial for UPS) is optimized.

Benefit: Robust thermal handling; Lower manufacturing cost vs. Composite.
3

Vacuum Hermetic Chamber

Operates at high vacuum (< 1Pa) to eliminate windage loss. The sealed environment makes it immune to Singapore's 90%+ humidity.

Benefit: Standby loss < 2kW; Immune to "Tropical Rot" (corrosion).

Part 4: Competitive Moat

Asymmetric Warfare Strategy

Competitive Landscape Radar

Analysis: While US/German competitors (Active Power, Piller) dominate brand awareness, they suffer from high initial costs and slower service response in ASEAN. HHE leverages the "China Tech + Singapore QC" hybrid model to offer superior TCO and service agility.

vs. Chemical Batteries (Li-ion)

The incumbent but flawed solution for high-power UPS.

  • Space: HHE saves 75% footprint (High value in Jurong).
  • Cooling: HHE needs no AC; Li-ion consumes 3-5% total power.
  • Life: HHE 20 years vs Li-ion 5-7 years (replacement cost).

vs. Global Flywheels (Active Power)

The premium incumbents.

  • Cost: HHE is ~40% cheaper due to supply chain.
  • Tech: HHE (Maglev) vs AP (Mech/Hybrid bearings). Lower maintenance.
  • Support: Direct local team vs Distributor model.

Supply Chain Resilience (JS-SEZ)

Utilizing the Johor-Singapore Special Economic Zone allows HHE to manufacture heavy components (rotors/enclosures) in Malaysia (low cost) while finishing integration in Singapore (high trust).
